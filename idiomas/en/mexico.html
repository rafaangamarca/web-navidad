CHRISTMAS IN MEXICO

Christmas in Mexico


Christmas in Mexico is celebrated from December 16 to January 6. It is a very family-centered, religious, and festive holiday that combines Catholic traditions with popular celebrations.



MAIN MEXICAN TRADITIONS

  • Las Posadas: celebrations from December 16 to 24 that recreate Joseph and Mary’s journey.
  • Piñatas: especially during posadas, filled with sweets.
  • Christmas Eve (Nochebuena): family dinner on the night of the 24th.
  • Midnight Mass (Misa de Gallo): midnight mass on Christmas Eve.
  • Three Kings Day: on January 6, when gifts arrive.


TRADITIONAL MEXICAN CHRISTMAS FOOD

  • Tamales: a traditional Christmas dish.
  • Bacalao a la vizcaína: very common at dinner.
  • Romeritos: served with mole and shrimp.
  • Pozole: typical at family gatherings.
  • Christmas punch (Ponche): a hot fruit drink.
  • Rosca de Reyes: the traditional cake for Three Kings Day.


CHRISTMAS CELEBRATION

Christmas in Mexico is joyful and community-focused. Posadas bring neighbors and families together with songs, food, and piñatas. On Christmas Eve, people share a big dinner and celebrate as a family, creating an atmosphere full of music, faith, and tradition.
