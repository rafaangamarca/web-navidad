CHRISTMAS IN GERMANY

Christmas in Germany


Christmas in Germany is mainly celebrated from December 24 to January 6. It is a traditional, family holiday, with Christmas markets, lights, carols, and gatherings to have dinner and open presents.



MAIN GERMAN TRADITIONS

  • Christmas markets (Weihnachtsmärkte): crafts, typical food, and drinks.
  • Advent: Advent wreaths and Advent calendars are used to count down to Christmas.
  • Christmas Eve (Heiligabend): special dinner and presents are opened.
  • Decorations: Christmas trees and lights all around the city.
  • Krampus and Nikolaustag: traditions for children, with gifts or surprises depending on behavior.


TRADITIONAL GERMAN CHRISTMAS FOOD

  • Stollen: sweet bread with dried fruits and marzipan.
  • Lebkuchen: gingerbread cookies.
  • Glühwein: spiced hot wine typical of Christmas markets.
  • Sausages, roast goose, and potatoes: main dishes at family dinners.
  • Marzipan and nuts: traditional sweets to enjoy.


CHRISTMAS CELEBRATION

German Christmas combines a family and festive atmosphere. Families decorate their homes, visit the markets, and enjoy special dinners on Christmas Eve. Children receive gifts, and Christmas music, carols, and lights create a cozy and cheerful mood.
