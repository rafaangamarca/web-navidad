CHRISTMAS IN JAPAN

Christmas in Japan


Christmas in Japan is not a religious holiday, but rather a commercial and social celebration. It is experienced more as a romantic event or something to enjoy with friends than as a traditional family gathering.



MAIN JAPANESE TRADITIONS

  • Romantic dates: Christmas is seen as a special day for couples.
  • Illuminations: cities are filled with spectacular lights and decorations.
  • Santa Claus: a popular figure, especially for children.
  • Gifts: people exchange small presents.
  • Western carols: international Christmas music in shops and streets.


TRADITIONAL JAPANESE CHRISTMAS FOOD

  • Fried chicken (KFC): the most famous “Christmas food” in Japan.
  • Christmas cake: sponge cake with whipped cream and strawberries.
  • Western-style meals: pizza or special dishes to celebrate.


CHRISTMAS CELEBRATION

Christmas in Japan is modern and light. People go out for dinner, walk around to see the illuminations, and enjoy the festive atmosphere. It is not an official public holiday and daily life continues, but with a special and cheerful touch.
