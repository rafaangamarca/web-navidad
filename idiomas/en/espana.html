CHRISTMAS IN SPAIN

Christmas in Spain


Christmas in Spain is celebrated with family, special meals, and traditions such as the Christmas Lottery, the New Year’s Eve “bell chimes”, and the Three Kings, when gifts are given.



MAIN SPANISH TRADITIONS

  • Christmas Lottery: held on December 22 and marks the official start of the holidays.
  • Christmas Eve and New Year’s Eve dinners: family gatherings with traditional food.
  • New Year’s Eve grapes: people eat twelve grapes at midnight for good luck.
  • Three Kings: on January 5 there are parades, and on January 6 gifts are given.
  • Roscón de Reyes: a traditional cake eaten on January 6.
  • Nativity scenes and Christmas carols: decorations and traditional songs.


TRADITIONAL SPANISH DISHES

  • Turrón: a very typical almond sweet for Christmas.
  • Polvorones and mantecados: traditional Christmas sweets.
  • Seafood: very common at festive dinners.
  • Roast lamb or suckling pig: main dishes in many families.
  • Stuffed turkey: common on Christmas Eve dinner.
  • Roscón de Reyes: traditional cake for Three Kings’ Day.


CHRISTMAS CELEBRATION

Christmas in Spain is mainly a family celebration. People get together for Christmas Eve dinner and celebrate New Year’s Eve together. Homes and streets are decorated with lights, Christmas trees, and nativity scenes.

During these dates there is a festive atmosphere, with Christmas carols, Christmas markets, and religious celebrations. The most anticipated moment for children is the arrival of the Three Kings, when gifts are received and the roscón is eaten.
