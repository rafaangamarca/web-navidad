CHRISTMAS IN BRAZIL

Christmas in Brazil


Christmas in Brazil is celebrated on December 24 and 25 and it happens during summer. It is a very family-oriented and joyful holiday, with big gatherings, music, and outdoor celebrations.



MAIN BRAZILIAN TRADITIONS

  • Ceia de Natal: family dinner on the night of December 24.
  • Misa do Galo: midnight mass on Christmas Eve.
  • Secret Santa (Amigo Secreto): gift exchange among family and friends.
  • Light decorations: streets and houses with lots of lights.
  • Santa Claus (Papai Noel): a popular figure among children.


TRADITIONAL BRAZILIAN CHRISTMAS FOOD

  • Roast turkey: the main Christmas dish.
  • Rice, farofa, and salads: traditional side dishes.
  • Salt cod (bacalhau): very common at Christmas dinner.
  • Rabanada: a typical dessert similar to French toast.
  • Tropical fruits and sweets: very common due to the warm weather.


CHRISTMAS CELEBRATION

Christmas in Brazil is festive and loud. Families gather around big tables, eat until midnight, and then exchange gifts. There is music, laughter, and a very lively atmosphere, mixing religious tradition with social celebration.
