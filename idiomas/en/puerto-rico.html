CHRISTMAS IN PUERTO RICO

Christmas in Puerto Rico


Christmas in Puerto Rico is one of the longest and happiest in the world. It starts in late November and can last until mid-January. It is known for music, parties, family, and a very festive atmosphere.



MAIN PUERTO RICAN TRADITIONS

  • Parrandas: surprise visits with Christmas music among friends and family.
  • Misas de aguinaldo: early-morning religious services before Christmas.
  • Christmas Eve and Christmas Day: big family gatherings.
  • Three Kings Day (January 6): children receive gifts.

  • Decorations: lights, nativity scenes, and colorful ornaments.


TRADITIONAL PUERTO RICAN FOOD

  • Roast pernil: the main Christmas dish.
  • Arroz con gandules: a traditional side dish.
  • Pasteles: dough filled with meat wrapped in plantain leaves.
  • Morcilla: a typical sausage.
  • Tembleque and arroz con dulce: traditional desserts.
  • Coquito: a coconut-based Christmas drink.


CHRISTMAS CELEBRATION

Christmas in Puerto Rico is very lively and social. Families and friends get together to eat, sing, and dance. There is constant music, outdoor celebrations, and a strong sense of community, mixing religious tradition with lots of joy and partying.
