CHRISTMAS IN UKRAINE

Christmas in Ukraine is mainly celebrated on January 7 according to the Orthodox calendar. It is a family and religious holiday, centered on Christmas Eve dinner and on keeping old traditions, such as carols and typical decorations.



MAIN UKRAINIAN TRADITIONS

  • Sviata Vecheria: traditional Christmas Eve dinner with 12 dishes.
  • Kolyadky: traditional Christmas carols.
  • Christmas decorations: trees, ornaments, and straw stars.
  • The first star: dinner begins when the first star appears in the sky.
  • Modest gifts: children receive small gifts, usually from family.


TRADITIONAL UKRAINIAN DISHES

  • Kutia: wheat with honey, poppy seeds, and dried fruits; the main dish of Sviata Vecheria.
  • Borsch: traditional beet soup.
  • Vareniki: dumplings filled with potato, cheese, or cabbage.
  • Fish and mushrooms: common dishes at Christmas Eve dinner.
  • Sauerkraut and traditional bread: typical side dishes.


CHRISTMAS CELEBRATION

Ukrainian Christmas is calm and family-centered, focused on faith and tradition. Families gather on Christmas Eve to share Sviata Vecheria, sing kolyadky, and decorate their homes. The celebration has a solemn but joyful atmosphere, combining respect for religion with family enjoyment.
